Every second counts when ambulances, firefighters, or police respond to emergencies. Yet in Nigeria‚Äîespecially Lagos‚Äîfixed-timer traffic lights delay priority vehicles. Studies report 200‚Äì227 vehicles/km in Lagos versus a national average of 11‚Äì16, with 25‚Äì30% of Nigeria‚Äôs vehicles operating on just 0.4% of its land area. üõ†Ô∏è What I Built

A smart traffic light system that detects emergency vehicles (ambulance, police, fire) in real time using AI and overrides signals instantly to clear a path. During normal conditions, it optimizes flow using real-time density analysis‚Äîno radios or manual control, just vision, logic, and speed.

üèóÔ∏è System Design

1. Vision & Sequential Scanning
A servo-mounted camera scans three approaches in 90¬∞ steps (Lane A ‚Üí B ‚Üí C), capturing a frame per lane per cycle.

2. AI Detection Engine: YOLOv11
Single-pass inference for low latency, trained to classify ambulance, police, fire truck, and civilian vehicles. Prototype trained on custom toy dataset; production requires real street data.

3. Smart Decision Logic
Dual-mode intelligence: emergency override and density optimization with safety-first rules (min green 10s, yellow 3s, all-red 2s, override buffer).

üß† Smart Decision Logic

üö® Emergency Override

Emergency detected in a lane ‚Üí wait minimum safety buffer ‚Üí turn that lane GREEN immediately ‚Üí hold all others RED ‚Üí monitor until clear ‚Üí resume normal operation. Multiple emergencies are handled first-come-first-served with automatic queuing.

üöó Density Optimization

If no emergency: count vehicles per lane, identify highest density, prioritize the busiest lane, then cycle others proportionally to reduce overall wait times.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Real Barrier: Data

The algorithms and architecture are ready, but AI is only as good as its training data. There are no open datasets for African emergency vehicles with the needed diversity (rain, night, motion blur, occlusion). Deployment in Nigeria requires partnerships with EMS, police, and fire services to curate continuous, real-world datasets and annotations.
